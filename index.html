Jordan A Viehmeyer

Jordan Viehmeyer is a 19 years old Software Engineer Student at Kennesaw State University. Ever since the age of five years old Jordan has been eager to learn everything and anything about computers. Jerry, Jordan's father bought him a desktop computer in 2003. He taught himself how to use the computer, and how to troubleshoot simple issues. On August 13th, 2007 Jordan and his father were involved in a motor vehicle accident killing his father and leaving Jordan alive. Jordan decdided that this was not the end of his talent, and only the beginning. Jordan, his mother Jennifer, and his sister Jenna moved to Canton, GA in February of 2008. Jordan has won many technology competitions at the Piedmont Regional Fair, and the Georgia State Fair. His current focus is to learn how to code from the ground up. Jordan has written an Android Application, and many other small programs.

Jordan's Achievements & Projects

  1. Age 13, entered in the Sequyah Regional Technology Fair now known as the "Piedmont Regional Technology Fair". There he entered into two categories: Digital Photography & The Computer Literacy Challenge. Jordan won first place in Computer Literacy and scored Second place in digital photography. He then entered into the Georgia State Technology Fair and there he placed first for Computer Literacy. He also gained a Microsoft Office Speacilist Certification.
  2. At the age of 14, he re-entered the technology fair and entered in both Project Programming & Computer Literacy. Jordan won second place in project programming and first place in Computer Literacy. After entering the State Technology fair he again placed first in the state.
  3. At the age of 15, Jordan became more ambitious. He started getting into robotics and programming. Jordan created a robot in which wrote his name with a sharpie marker with absolute precision. He also created a simple Visual Basic calculator. Both of his projects this year won the regional fair, and then also won the state fair.
  4. At the age of 16, Jordan wanted to take his robotics skills to the next level. He built a robot that would go through a maze completely and with an algortithm simplifying the robots movements into a straight shot throughout the maze. 
  5. At Jordan's high school there was no such club for his interests, so he decided to start his own club. He built it from the ground up managing the members and club officers for three years in a row until he graduated in May of 2016.
